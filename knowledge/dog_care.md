# Dog Care Guide

## Feeding

Feeding is a core daily care task for dogs. PawPal should usually treat feeding as a high-priority recurring task because dogs benefit from consistent routines.

For scheduling purposes:
- Feeding is usually high priority.
- Feeding usually takes 10 to 15 minutes.
- Feeding can be scheduled in the morning, evening, or both depending on the owner's routine.
- Puppies may need more frequent meals than adult dogs.
- Some adult dogs do better with two smaller meals instead of one large meal.

PawPal should not recommend a specific diet, portion size, or supplement plan. Diet decisions depend on age, breed, activity level, weight, and health status.

## Water

Fresh water should be available throughout the day. Water bowl checks are simple but important daily tasks.

For scheduling purposes:
- Water refill or water check is usually high priority.
- This task usually takes 2 to 5 minutes.
- It can be scheduled anytime.
- PawPal can group it near feeding tasks.

## Walking and Exercise

Dogs need exercise for physical health and mental stimulation. Exercise needs vary depending on breed, age, sex, and health.

For scheduling purposes:
- Walking is usually medium to high priority.
- A normal walk task can be estimated at 20 to 45 minutes.
- Short potty breaks can be estimated at 5 to 10 minutes.
- Walks are often best scheduled in the morning or evening.
- If the user has limited time, feeding, water, and medication should usually be scheduled before long walks.

PawPal should not assume every dog needs the same amount of exercise. Older dogs, puppies, injured dogs, and some breeds may need adjusted activity.

## Grooming

Grooming helps keep dogs clean and can reduce shedding. Brushing can be scheduled more often than bathing.

For scheduling purposes:
- Brushing is usually low to medium priority.
- Brushing can take 10 to 20 minutes.
- Bathing is usually lower urgency unless the dog is dirty or has a specific need.
- Bathing can take 30 to 60 minutes.
- PawPal should schedule grooming after higher-priority daily care tasks.

PawPal should avoid telling users to bathe dogs too frequently. Bathing needs vary by coat, skin condition, and lifestyle.

## Flea and Tick Check

Dogs may need flea and tick checks, especially after outdoor activity or during warm seasons.

For scheduling purposes:
- Flea and tick checks are usually medium priority.
- They usually take 5 to 10 minutes.
- They can be scheduled after walks or outdoor play.
- If a user mentions fleas, ticks, wounds, or skin irritation, PawPal should suggest contacting a veterinarian if symptoms persist.

## Medication Reminders

Medication tasks should be treated as high priority. PawPal can help schedule reminders, but it must not give dosage instructions or medical recommendations.

For scheduling purposes:
- Medication is high priority.
- Medication reminders usually take 5 to 10 minutes.
- Medication tasks should not be skipped just because lower-priority tasks take too much time.
- If the user mentions medication, pills, dosage, injury, vomiting, seizure, blood, breathing problems, or emergency, PawPal must show a veterinary disclaimer.

Required disclaimer:
This app helps with scheduling only and is not a substitute for professional veterinary advice.

## Priority Rules for Dogs

When time is limited, PawPal should prioritize dog tasks roughly in this order:

1. Medication or urgent health-related reminders
2. Water
3. Feeding
4. Short potty break
5. Walk or exercise
6. Flea/tick check
7. Grooming
8. Optional play or enrichment

PawPal should explain when a lower-priority task is skipped because available time is limited.

## Safety Notes

PawPal should recommend contacting a veterinarian or emergency clinic if the user mentions:
- Seizure
- Collapse
- Difficulty breathing
- Heavy bleeding
- Poison or toxin ingestion
- Severe vomiting
- Severe injury
- Extreme lethargy
- Unusual behavior that does not improve

PawPal should not diagnose disease, prescribe treatment, or recommend medication dosage.
