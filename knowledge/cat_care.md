# Cat Care Guide

## Feeding

Feeding is a core daily care task for cats. Cats benefit from consistent feeding routines, and food choice depends on age, activity level, and health.

For scheduling purposes:
- Feeding is usually high priority.
- Feeding usually takes 5 to 10 minutes.
- Feeding can be scheduled in the morning, evening, or both.
- PawPal should not recommend specific medical diets, supplements, or portion sizes.

If a cat refuses food or shows vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, or other concerning symptoms, PawPal should suggest contacting a veterinarian.

## Water

Cats should have access to fresh, clean water. Water bowl checks are small but important care tasks.

For scheduling purposes:
- Water check or refill is high priority.
- This task usually takes 2 to 5 minutes.
- It can be scheduled anytime.
- It can be grouped with feeding.

## Litter Box Cleaning

Indoor cats need a clean litter box in an accessible location. A dirty litter box can cause stress and may lead cats to avoid using it.

For scheduling purposes:
- Scooping litter is usually high priority.
- Scooping usually takes 5 to 10 minutes.
- Scooping should usually be treated as a daily recurring task.
- Full litter replacement and washing can be scheduled as a weekly or periodic task.
- In a multi-level home, cats may need access to more than one litter box.

If the user says a cat suddenly refuses to use the litter box, PawPal should include a note that this can sometimes be related to health or stress and may require veterinary advice.

## Playtime and Enrichment

Playtime supports exercise and mental enrichment. It is useful, but it is usually more flexible than feeding, water, medication, or litter cleaning.

For scheduling purposes:
- Playtime is usually medium priority.
- Playtime usually takes 10 to 20 minutes.
- It can be scheduled anytime.
- If time is limited, playtime can be shortened before feeding, water, medication, or litter cleaning are skipped.

## Grooming

Most cats keep themselves clean, but brushing can help reduce shedding and hairballs.

For scheduling purposes:
- Brushing is usually low to medium priority.
- Brushing usually takes 5 to 15 minutes.
- Nail trimming can be scheduled periodically.
- Bathing is usually not a normal frequent task for most cats unless there is a specific reason.

PawPal should not force grooming tasks above urgent care needs.

## Scratching and Environment

Cats need appropriate scratching surfaces. Scratching supports normal behavior and claw maintenance.

For scheduling purposes:
- Replacing or checking scratching surfaces is low priority.
- Environmental enrichment tasks are usually flexible.
- These tasks can be scheduled when required daily care is already complete.

## Medication Reminders

Medication reminders for cats are high priority. PawPal can schedule medication tasks but must not provide diagnosis, dosage, or treatment instructions.

For scheduling purposes:
- Medication is high priority.
- Medication reminders usually take 5 to 10 minutes.
- If medication is mentioned, PawPal should show a veterinary disclaimer.

Required disclaimer:
This app helps with scheduling only and is not a substitute for professional veterinary advice.

## Priority Rules for Cats

When time is limited, PawPal should prioritize cat tasks roughly in this order:

1. Medication or urgent health-related reminders
2. Water
3. Feeding
4. Litter box cleaning
5. Playtime
6. Grooming
7. Scratching/environment tasks

PawPal should explain when a lower-priority task is skipped because available time is limited.

## Safety Notes

PawPal should recommend contacting a veterinarian or emergency clinic if the user mentions:
- Seizure
- Collapse
- Difficulty breathing
- Heavy bleeding
- Poison or toxin ingestion
- Repeated vomiting
- Extreme lethargy
- Refusing food for an extended period
- Sudden litter box avoidance
- Severe injury

PawPal should not diagnose disease, prescribe treatment, or recommend medication dosage.
